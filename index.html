Introduction

Extreme weather phenomena (such as tornadoes, waterspouts, lightnings, hails, storms) are a major threat to public health, causing material damage, injuries, and even deaths. Often the extreme weather phenomena interact and produce a complex process. Over the last decades weather-related disasters have attracted the scientific interest due to their significant socioeconomic impact. In addition, recent scientific papers show that climate change will affect the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events. A possible increase in extreme effects and negative impacts requires a systematic inventory and monitoring of the phenomena, both to mitigate these impacts and to develop appropriate risk and disaster management tools and models. Greece experiences a variety of extreme weather phenomena, often followed by negative impacts on socio-economic activity. However, the study focuses mainly on certain areas of the country, and on particular weather phenomena. Below we will analyze the selected phenomena and see a few time animations, but first we will see the seasonal variability of the phenomena and a timeline event.

Seasonal variability of extreme events

The findings of the weather events revealed a specific spatial and seasonal distribution over Greece, within the 2010-2018 period. The following figure, summarises these findings with extreme weather events during each season.

Seasonal variability of severe weather phenomena over Greece 2010-2018

The largest number of extreme weather events was recorded during autumn (258), followed by summer (191), winter (165) and spring (149). The seasonal variability of lightning events over Greece shows that autumn and summer seasons are the more prominent for lightning activity. Autumn is characterized as the most active cyclone season, as low pressure systems over the west Greece are interacting with the warm sea body of the Ionian Sea, producing unstable weather conditions that propagate eastward over the Aegean Sea. During summer season lightning activity is related to unstable weather conditions due to thermal convection over the mainland Greece as thunderstorms are of continental origin. These unstable conditions appear during the warmest hours of the day.

Flash Floods

Flash floods are break out suddenly and are the result of heavy rainfall, extreme storms, siphons or even glacier melting water. Floods result in the loss of human lives, destruction of buildings and structures, road network, sewerage systems and electricity transmission networks. The destruction of these infrastructures can lead to disruption of drinking water supply, transport and communication difficulties, the occurrence of diseases related to drinking water quality and difficulties in healthcare.

Flash floods mainly occur during the autumn period, and they occur mainly along coastal regions and in close proximity of large city centres. High density residential development increases the number of individuals and properties at risk. Below we can see recorded Flash Floods events from 2010-2018.

Tornadic Events

Tornado is a rapidly rotating windmill that is in contact with both the ground and the base of boulders. Pitch spiders are a category of clouds with very high vertical growth, and are associated with severe weather phenomena. Droplets are rotated at speeds of 30 m /s. up to 135 m / s. while their diameter does not exceed 2 km. They can have several shapes, but their classical appearance is the elephant provocase that escapes from the base of the pits.Below we can see recorded Tornadic events from 2010-2018

Lightnings

Thunderbolts are atmospheric electricity evacuation accompanied by thunder, and is considered to be one of the most powerful and impressive phenomena that occur in the atmosphere. These cloud discharges can be Cloud to Cloud-CtC, Cloud to Air CtA, or Cloud to Ground-CtG.They have a particular place in the scientific literature as they can cause deaths, injuries and damage.Below we can see recorded lightning events that caused any form of damage or death from 2010-2018
